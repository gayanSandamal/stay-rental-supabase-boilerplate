# Easy Rent — User & Admin Flows

A walkthrough of the three core journeys in Easy Rent, illustrated with real
screenshots captured by Playwright:

1. **Renter** (public / tenant) — discover, filter, and contact a landlord.
2. **Landlord** — list and manage a property.
3. **Admin / Ops** — back-office: approvals, business accounts, settings, visibility.

> **How these were captured.** Screenshots are generated by `e2e/capture-flows.spec.ts`
> (Playwright, real Chromium) against a **locally seeded** Easy Rent stack — the live
> site is not used because its documented seed accounts are all `tenant`-role (so the
> back-office renders empty) and its marketplace is currently unpopulated. The local
> seed provides ~400 sample listings (254 active) across 26 landlords / 10 business
> accounts, plus properly-roled `admin` / `landlord` / `tenant` accounts, so these
> views reflect a realistic, populated app. To regenerate, see
> [Regenerating the screenshots](#regenerating-the-screenshots).

## 1. Renter flow

The renter journey requires **no login** — browsing and contacting landlords is open
to everyone. (A signed-in tenant gets the same experience; after sign-in a `tenant`
is redirected to `/listings` — see [Role redirects](#role-redirects).)

### 1.1 Land on the homepage (`/`)

The marketing homepage leads with the value proposition (verified, mid-to-long-term
Sri Lanka rentals) and two primary CTAs — **Browse Listings** and **List Your Property**.

![Easy Rent homepage](./screenshots/01-home.png)

### 1.2 Browse listings (`/listings`)

The **Available Rentals** index shows the search bar, a **Filters** control, and a grid
of listing cards. Each card surfaces title, location, bedrooms, rent (LKR/month),
publisher (individual vs business), and verification/status badges.

![Listings index](./screenshots/02-listings-index.png)

### 1.3 Filter results

Filters are reflected in the URL (e.g. `/listings?city=Colombo`), so a filtered view is
shareable and bookmarkable. Rental-specific filters include city/district, price range,
bedrooms, property type, power backup, water source, fiber, parking, pets, and
verified/visited flags.

![Filtered listings](./screenshots/03-listings-filtered.png)

### 1.4 View a listing (`/listings/[id]`)

The detail page shows the photo gallery, description, property details, and the
Sri-Lanka-specific resilience fields (power backup, water source, fiber, AC/fans),
safety features, and a sticky pricing/contact sidebar.

![Listing detail](./screenshots/04-listing-detail.png)

### 1.5 Contact the landlord

Contact is **direct** — phone / WhatsApp, no in-app messaging or booking engine.
Verified contact numbers expose **Call** and **WhatsApp** actions.

![Contact the landlord](./screenshots/05-listing-contact.png)

### 1.6 Mobile

The renter experience is responsive end-to-end.

![Listings on mobile](./screenshots/06-listings-mobile.png)

## 2. Landlord flow

Listings are **free and unlimited** on every plan — Easy Rent monetises *visibility*,
not supply. A `tenant` is **auto-upgraded to `landlord`** on creating their first
listing, and new listings start as **`pending`** until ops/admin approve them.

### 2.1 Sign in (`/sign-in`)

![Sign-in form](./screenshots/10-signin.png)

### 2.2 Dashboard overview (`/dashboard`)

After sign-in a landlord lands on the dashboard: active/verified listing stats and the
left-nav (Overview, Listings, Analytics, Saved Alerts, Settings, Security).

![Landlord dashboard](./screenshots/11-dashboard-overview.png)

### 2.3 Manage listings (`/dashboard/listings`)

The landlord sees **only their own** listings (admin/ops see more). Note: a landlord
does **not** see approve/reject controls — those are admin-only.

![Manage listings](./screenshots/12-dashboard-listings.png)

### 2.4 Create a listing (`/dashboard/listings/new`)

The config-driven create form collects title, address, city, bedrooms, rent, contact
number(s), and the resilience fields. On submit the listing is created with status
**`pending`**.

![New listing form](./screenshots/13-new-listing-form.png)

### 2.5 Listing detail & visibility (landlord view)

On their own listing, a landlord sees **request** buttons for the visibility products
(Boost / Featured / Urgent / bundles). Landlords **cannot self-activate** these —
activation is manual and admin/ops-only after offline payment.

![Landlord listing detail](./screenshots/14-listing-detail-dashboard.png)

## 3. Admin / Ops flow

The back-office (`/back-office/**`) is gated to **`admin`** and **`ops`** by
`requireBackOfficeAccess()`.

### 3.1 Back-office overview (`/back-office`)

Stat cards for business accounts, active team members, and business listings.

![Back-office overview](./screenshots/20-backoffice-overview.png)

### 3.2 Business accounts (`/back-office/business-accounts`)

List of agency/corporate accounts; create new ones and manage their members.

![Business accounts](./screenshots/21-business-accounts.png)

![New business account](./screenshots/22-business-account-new.png)

### 3.3 Team members (`/back-office/team-members`)

Members across business accounts, with their account-scoped role (owner/admin/member).

![Team members](./screenshots/23-team-members.png)

### 3.4 Platform-wide listings (`/back-office/listings`)

Every listing across all landlords/business accounts (not limited to one owner).

![Back-office listings](./screenshots/24-backoffice-listings.png)

### 3.5 Feature-flag settings (`/back-office/settings`)

Admins toggle runtime feature flags here (persisted to the `feature_flags` table and
audit-logged). This includes the **Paid visibility / pricing** master switch — **OFF by
default**, so the platform presents as fully free until paid visibility launches.

![Feature flag settings](./screenshots/25-settings-feature-flags.png)

![Feature flag toggled](./screenshots/28-feature-flag-toggled.png)

### 3.6 Listing approval / rejection (`/dashboard/listings/[id]`)

Approvals happen on the listing detail page (not the back-office). Admin/ops see
**Approve** (→ `active` + verified), **Mark Visited**, a **status** dropdown, and
**Reject** (opens a modal to pick a reason → `rejected` + stored reason).

![Approval controls](./screenshots/26-approval-form.png)

![Reject modal](./screenshots/27-reject-modal.png)

### 3.7 Visibility activation (Boost / Featured / Urgent)

Visibility activation is **admin API-only** (`POST /api/listings/[id]/{boost|feature|urgent|bundle}`),
performed after offline payment is confirmed. A landlord requesting visibility from
their dashboard (§2.5) gets a `403` if they try to self-activate. Active visibility
drives the search ranking: **Featured → Boost → Urgent → plan tier → verified →
completeness → newest**.

## Regenerating the screenshots

With a seeded local stack running and `pnpm dev` serving `http://localhost:3000`:

```bash
BASE_URL=http://localhost:3000 \
ADMIN_EMAIL=admin@easyrent.com ADMIN_PASSWORD=admin123 \
LANDLORD_EMAIL=landlord@test.com LANDLORD_PASSWORD=landlord123 \
TENANT_EMAIL=tenant@test.com TENANT_PASSWORD=tenant123 \
ALLOW_MUTATION=1 pnpm exec playwright test e2e/capture-flows.spec.ts --project=desktop
```

Screenshots are written to `docs/screenshots/` with stable names, so re-running
overwrites them in place and this document stays in sync. The capture spec is
best-effort: any optional control that isn't present is skipped (logged as `[SKIP]`)
rather than failing the run.
