
# Easy Rent Platform — Monetization Plan & Strategy
## Reimagined Around **Free Listing + Paid Visibility**

---

## 1. Executive Summary

The most practical and proven model for Easy Rent is:

**Let landlords list for free, then monetize visibility, urgency, and scale.**

That means Easy Rent should not try to make its first revenue primarily from renters, deposits, or complex subscription flows. Instead, it should use a simple marketplace engine that works well in the Sri Lankan property space:

- **Free listing** to attract supply quickly
- **Paid visibility** to monetize from day zero
- **Low-friction add-ons** that landlords understand immediately
- **A small number of optional paid landlord plans** only after traction starts

This model is the strongest fit because:

- it reduces friction for new landlords
- it helps Easy Rent compete with ikman.lk and LankaPropertyWeb
- it creates revenue without needing full online transaction infrastructure
- it matches your current tech stack and manual ops model
- it keeps the platform affordable while covering operating costs from the beginning

---

## 2. Business Objective

Easy Rent must achieve **three goals at the same time**:

1. **Build inventory fast**
2. **Generate revenue from day zero**
3. **Compete on trust, rental focus, and better visibility tools**

The platform should position itself as:

- **Sri Lanka’s rental-first marketplace**
- **trusted and verified**
- **direct and simple**
- **better than generic classifieds for landlords who want real inquiries**

---

## 3. Why “Free Listing + Paid Visibility” Is the Best Model

For this business domain, landlords care about one thing more than anything else:

> “Can I get more serious tenants quickly?”

They do **not** initially care about complex dashboards, deep analytics, or advanced SaaS-style features. They care about:

- visibility
- speed
- better response rate
- easier contact through phone and WhatsApp
- trust

So the easiest way to make money is not to overcharge for posting.
It is to allow posting freely and then sell:

- **Boosts**
- **featured placement**
- **urgent visibility**
- **premium landlord exposure**
- **multi-listing scale plans**

That is why the primary monetization engine should be **visibility products**, not complicated subscriptions.

## 5. Recommended Pricing Model

## 5.1 Landlord Pricing — Core Strategy

### A. Free Listing
**Price:** Free

**Best for:** first-time landlords, casual landlords, individuals

**Includes:**
- **unlimited active listings**
- direct phone and WhatsApp contact
- standard visibility
- 30-day listing duration
- manual approval / verification workflow

**Purpose:**
- maximize listing supply
- make it easy to try the platform
- remove all barriers to listing

This should remain the main acquisition tool.

---

### B. Boost Listing
**Price:** **LKR 250 for 7 days**

**Best for:** all landlords

**Includes:**
- improved ranking in search results
- “Boosted” label
- more exposure for one listing
- available even to Free users

**Purpose:**
- easiest day-zero monetization tool
- very easy to explain and sell
- ideal for urgent vacancies

This is the first and most important revenue product.

---

### C. Featured Listing
**Price:** **LKR 500 for 7 days**

**Best for:** landlords who want maximum exposure

**Includes:**
- top placement in relevant category pages
- stronger homepage/category visibility
- featured badge
- one listing only

**Purpose:**
- premium visibility layer above Boost
- higher-value upsell for agents and urgent landlords
- better monetization of high-demand locations

This should be limited so it feels premium.

---

### D. Urgent Badge Add-On
**Price:** **LKR 150 for 7 days**

**Best for:** landlords who want quick action

**Includes:**
- “Urgent” badge on listing
- visibility enhancement in cards
- can be sold alone or bundled with Boost

**Purpose:**
- low-cost microtransaction
- easy upsell for landlords with urgent vacancies

---

### E. Starter Landlord Pack
**Price:** **LKR 900/month**

**Best for:** landlords with a few properties

**Includes:**
- **unlimited active listings**
- 1 free Boost per month
- higher visibility than Free
- renewal reminders
- better dashboard management

**Purpose:**
- first subscription step for repeat landlords
- should be sold as convenience + better exposure + included Boosts

This is useful, but it should be **secondary** to paid visibility in your launch messaging.

---

### F. Pro Landlord Pack
**Price:** **LKR 2,500/month**

**Best for:** serious landlords and small agents

**Includes:**
- **unlimited active listings**
- 3 free Boosts per month
- featured priority consideration
- listing performance summary
- faster review / support
- custom profile page

**Purpose:**
- monetize repeat supply-side users
- better value for landlords with many properties

---

### G. Agency Pack
**Price:** **LKR 5,000/month**

**Best for:** agencies and larger portfolio owners

**Includes:**
- **unlimited active listings**
- 6 Boosts per month
- agency badge
- team / business account support
- priority ops support
- bulk renewals

**Purpose:**
- capture agencies and heavy portfolio owners
- best value for volume landlords

---

## 5.2 Renter Pricing — Deprioritized at Launch

### Free Renter Access
**Price:** Free

**Includes:**
- browse all listings
- contact landlords directly
- save basic searches
- receive normal alerts

### Premium Renter Access
**Price:** **LKR 300/month**  
**Status:** optional, but **not a primary launch revenue engine**

**Includes:**
- early access to selected listings
- unlimited saved alerts
- premium notifications
- future premium search tools

### Recommendation
Keep renter premium available if already built, but do **not** rely on it for early revenue.
At launch, renters should mostly remain frictionless so demand grows quickly.

---

## 6. Final Revenue Engine Priority

Easy Rent should prioritize revenue in this order:

1. **Boost Listing**
2. **Featured Listing**
3. **Starter / Pro / Agency landlord packs**
4. **Urgent badge and bundle add-ons**
5. **Renter premium**
6. **Future transaction-based services**

That means your pitch to landlords should be:

> List free. Pay only when you want more visibility, faster results, or more active listings.

That is much easier to sell than:
> Pay monthly before you know whether the platform works.

## 8. Break-Even Scenarios

Easy Rent needs approximately **LKR 48,000/month** to cover its baseline costs.

Here are practical ways to reach break-even:

### Option A — Only Boosts
- 192 Boosts per month × LKR 250 = **LKR 48,000**

### Option B — Mixed Visibility Sales
- 80 Boosts × 250 = **LKR 20,000**
- 30 Featured listings × 500 = **LKR 15,000**
- 20 Urgent add-ons × 150 = **LKR 3,000**
- 10 Starter Packs × 900 = **LKR 9,000**
- **Total = LKR 47,000**

### Option C — More Subscription Heavy
- 20 Starter Packs × 900 = **LKR 18,000**
- 8 Pro Packs × 2,500 = **LKR 20,000**
- 20 Boosts × 250 = **LKR 5,000**
- 10 Featured listings × 500 = **LKR 5,000**
- **Total = LKR 48,000**

The most realistic early path is **Option B**:
a mix of free supply acquisition plus paid visibility sales.

## 9. Recommended Listing Ranking Logic

To support monetization without destroying trust, listings should rank in this order:

1. active Featured listings
2. active Boost listings
3. Pro / Agency plan listings
4. verified and complete listings
5. newest standard listings

This creates a fair system where:
- landlords can pay for more visibility
- listing quality still matters
- the marketplace does not feel fake or manipulated

## 11. Marketing Strategy to Match This Model

Since the monetization model is visibility-based, marketing should focus on **bringing in landlords first**, then converting them into paid exposure.

### Month 0–2: Supply Acquisition
The new marketing + ops hire should focus on:

- calling landlords and agents already advertising on other platforms
- helping them create their first free listing
- offering easy onboarding support
- explaining the free listing + boost option clearly
- collecting photos and listing information fast
- creating TikTok, Facebook, Instagram, and short video posts using listings already on the platform

### Month 1–3: Revenue Conversion
Once listings are active, the ops person should:

- monitor listings with low views
- contact landlords and suggest Boost / Featured upgrades
- push visibility packs in high-demand areas
- target landlords with urgent vacancies
- promote area pages such as Colombo, Kandy, Negombo, Galle, Jaffna

### Month 3–6: Trust + Retention
- landlord success stories
- tenant testimonials
- verified landlord badges
- repeat listing offers
- bundle promotions for agencies and repeat advertisers

---

## 12. Daily Tasks for Marketing + Ops Hire

To make this strategy work, the hired person should produce daily results, not just activity.

### Daily Tasks
- call or message new landlords / agents
- onboard free listings
- follow up on unapproved or incomplete listings
- recommend Boost / Featured products
- create social content from live listings
- maintain listing quality and moderation
- respond to landlord questions
- track simple sales metrics

### Daily KPI Targets
- **10–20 landlord outreach attempts**
- **2–5 new free listings onboarded**
- **1–3 paid visibility sales**
- **1–2 short-form content pieces posted**
- **all pending listing approvals handled quickly**

This role is not just marketing.
It is **growth + onboarding + monetization ops**.

---

## 13. What Easy Rent Should Not Do at Launch

To stay lean and profitable, Easy Rent should **not** depend on these too early:

- complicated subscription structures
- escrow / deposit handling
- furniture rental operations
- maintenance service operations
- heavy ad network dependence
- large discount or coupon programs
- complex payment automation before demand is proven

These can come later.
Right now the business should focus on:

- listings
- visibility
- trust
- landlord acquisition
- paid exposure

This strategy fits the current platform well.

### Already aligned with the tech stack
- landlord plans exist
- Boost is already implemented
- manual activation by admin / ops is already possible
- alerts and reminders exist
- email infrastructure exists

- keep engineering cost lower
